Welcome to **strAIght up!** — a hybrid hackathon designed for young builders and founders ready to tackle pressing real-world problems through the power of AI.

Over the course of a week, participants go from idea to working demo, guided by experienced mentors, supported by a driven community, and equipped with the AI tools to make it happen. This is the place where curiosity meets technology, and ideas become reality.

Everything you need for the event is right here.

1. Pick your **challenge theme** (we have 25 pretty specific challenge statements to choose from) from here: [Challenge Statements](https://www.notion.so/Challenge-Statements-33c709c9468280828a04e957fac782c4?pvs=21)

    > ⚠️ *We're issuing AI-forward challenges — don't try and retrofit your existing idea into it.*

2. Start ideating — we're looking for challenge statement-solution fit here
3. Make your demo + deck

> 🛠️ **AI is load-bearing, not optional.** Every solution must use AI in a non-trivial way — a multi-step pipeline, agentic workflow, RAG with evaluation, tool-use with state, multimodal ingest, or equivalent. A "thin wrapper" or single Claude API call fails Technological Execution (33% of score). Solutions without meaningful AI fail Challenge-Solution Fit (another 33%) since the challenges themselves are AI-framed. Target: at least one non-trivial AI primitive that is doing real load-bearing work, embedded in a 5–7 step pipeline you can draw on a whiteboard.

## Challenge themes

> Each challenge has the official statement + HMW (from the source), followed by **Archetype** (a named SEA user to ideate for) and **Done to death** (default ideas to skip). Archetype and Done-to-death are strategic additions, not source material.

### Theme A: Structural Shifts (Macro)

#### A1. Ambient AI agents and trust
As personal AI agents begin acting on behalf of users across finance, communication, and operations, failures will no longer be limited to bad recommendations. They could trigger financial loss, privacy breaches, or unsafe decisions. **How might we build trust, permissions, and fail-safes into ambient personal AI agents before they become default interfaces to daily life?**

**Archetype:** Daniel, 34, freelance designer in Tiong Bahru. Lets his AI agent handle Grab bookings, reply to Carousell DMs, and auto-pay utility bills. Lost S$400 last month when the agent clicked a phishing link in an email thread.

**Done to death:** generic agent-permissions dashboard; another "AI wallet" with security branding; blockchain agent audit logs; consent pop-ups for every action; "AI firewall" with no product.

#### A2. Fragile authenticity in a synthetic world
As AI-generated content becomes indistinguishable from human output, trust in hiring, support, identity, and communication systems weakens. Verification becomes a foundational layer, not a premium feature. **How might we design lightweight authenticity and provenance systems that help people verify what is real, human, and trustworthy in everyday interactions?**

**Archetype:** Priya, 29, HR manager at an SME in Tanjong Pagar. Screens ~40 CVs a week; can no longer tell which cover letters are AI-generated. Two recent hires had AI-fabricated portfolios.

**Done to death:** generic AI-content detector; browser extension that red-flags "suspicious" images; NFT identity; standalone deepfake-detection website; watermarking scheme with no distribution.

#### A3. Unequal outcomes from AI personalisation
AI personalisation may improve convenience for everyone, but not necessarily outcomes. Higher-income users may receive better decisions, better health advice, and better learning paths, while lower-income users get lower-quality automation at scale. **How might we ensure AI personalisation improves outcomes for underserved users, not just efficiency for those already advantaged?**

**Archetype:** Auntie Rohana, 52, office cleaner in Raffles Place. Uses a free AI assistant that gives her generic career advice; her nephew (bank analyst) uses a $30/mo tier that helped him negotiate a pay bump. Same technology, opposite outcomes.

**Done to death:** "AI for the poor" skin on a generic chatbot; a minority-language GPT wrapper with no grounding; a chatbot that "explains bureaucratic forms" (a dozen exist); another benefits-finder that can't actually file.

#### A4. Ageing, isolation, and independent living
As populations age and more people live alone for longer, safety, care, and autonomy become core social infrastructure issues. The challenge is not just monitoring older adults, but supporting dignity while reducing risk. **How might we support safe, dignified independent living for ageing populations without making care feel invasive or dehumanising?**

**Archetype:** Mdm Lim, 78, widow, 4-room HDB in Toa Payoh. Two adult children overseas (Shanghai, London). Manages her own CareShield and MediSave, but is starting to forget medications and hasn't told her kids.

**Done to death:** fall-detection wearable; generic eldercare chatbot; another medication-reminder app; video-call app "designed for seniors"; smart-home voice assistant repackaged.

#### A5. AI Adoption Gap Becomes Power Gap *(Powered by AIF)*
If AI becomes the productivity multiplier of the decade — and boys experiment earlier and more aggressively — income and influence gaps could widen. **How might we design AI learning environments that increase experimentation confidence among teenage girls before self-selection away from tech begins?**

**Archetype:** Aisyah, 15, Sec 3 at an IP school in the East. Uses ChatGPT for homework but has never *built* with it. Her male classmates are shipping Discord bots. Her mother thinks AI is "not for girls."

**Done to death:** "AI for girls" re-skinned ChatGPT; pink-branded coding bootcamp; a "female founder quotes" feed; another Scratch-like block editor with no AI.

### Theme B: Work, Income & Capability

#### B1. Slow conversion from skills to income
Many people can learn skills online, but converting those skills into paid work still takes too long. The bottleneck is not knowledge acquisition, but market access, trust, and proof of ability. **How might we help people convert newly learned skills into real income within weeks instead of years?**

**Archetype:** Raj, 24, self-taught full-stack dev, ITE background, lives in Tampines. Finished 6 Udemy courses, built 2 portfolio sites, still has not landed a paid gig after 4 months of applying.

**Done to death:** Upwork clone; another course marketplace; "LinkedIn but fairer"; AI-matched job aggregator (done 100×).

#### B2. Lack of low-friction entry points to earning
For many young people, especially students and early-career workers, earning opportunities still depend on resumes, interviews, and prior experience. This creates a catch-22 for those trying to get started. **How might we create legitimate, low-friction ways for people to start earning without relying on resumes, formal credentials, or prior job history?**

**Archetype:** Fariz, 19, NITEC grad. Doing food delivery while studying; wants S$500/month doing something that uses his brain, not his bike. No resume, no portfolio, no network.

**Done to death:** MTurk-style microtask platform; TikTok-style "skills feed"; generic gig aggregator; another "resume-free job board" that still asks for a resume.

#### B3. Weak connection between learning and real work
Most education systems still separate learning from practical application. Learners accumulate credentials, but not evidence of value creation in real settings. **How might we connect learning directly to projects, clients, and measurable outputs so capability is built through real work?**

**Archetype:** Jia En, 21, NUS CS Year 3. CAP of 4.8. Zero deployed code anyone outside a classroom has used.

**Done to death:** project-based learning platform (Riipen, Parker Dewey exist); another "build in public" tracker; yet another bootcamp clone; capstone-project matchmaker that never hands off work.

#### B4. Entry-level work is being compressed by AI
Many traditional stepping-stone roles in admin, coordination, support, and junior knowledge work are becoming less available as AI automates repetitive tasks. This removes common entry points into careers. **How might we create new AI-leveraged pathways for early-career workers to build proof of ability and income in a labor market with fewer traditional entry roles?**

**Archetype:** Marcus, 23, just graduated SMU Business. Entry-level analyst roles at banks have halved because agents do the Excel work. He's applying to roles that don't exist yet.

**Done to death:** "AI copilot for junior analysts" (still doesn't create the role); broad upskilling platform for displaced workers; AI-interview-prep tool; another career-pivot coach.

#### B5. Opportunity access is socially gated *(Powered by AIF)*
Exposure to internships, mentors, projects, and jobs is often shaped by networks, school brand, and insider access rather than raw capability. This prevents many capable people from ever getting surfaced. **How might we redesign opportunity discovery so access is driven less by social capital and pedigree, and more by demonstrated potential and readiness?**

**Archetype:** Hui Ling, 22, SUSS student, rental flat in Bedok. Her portfolio is stronger than most of the ACS-SMU pipeline her professors mentor — but that pipeline gets the internship intros, not her.

**Done to death:** generic mentorship-matching app; "blind hiring" that just hides names on resumes; network-graph visualizer; yet another alumni-engagement SaaS.

### Theme C: Attention, Agency & Coordination

#### C1. Priority collapse in high-noise environments
People are overwhelmed by tasks, notifications, deadlines, and messages, but the deeper problem is not volume alone. It is the inability to distinguish what is urgent from what is actually important. **How might we help people identify and act on their highest-priority work in environments where everything competes for attention?**

**Archetype:** Wei Ming, 31, PM at a Series B startup. 47 unread Slacks, 12 Jira tickets, 8 meeting invites. Cannot tell which of the six "urgent" things is actually urgent.

**Done to death:** todo app with "AI priority score" (Motion owns this); another inbox-zero tool; pomodoro timer with AI; "top 3 things today" card generator.

#### C2. Decision fatigue from overlapping responsibilities
Students, workers, and caregivers often operate across multiple roles at once, leading to silent cognitive overload. Existing productivity tools track tasks, but do little to reduce mental fragmentation. **How might we reduce cognitive overload for people juggling multiple responsibilities, deadlines, and roles at the same time?**

**Archetype:** Jun Hao, 28, part-time master's + full-time job + caregiving for his grandfather with dementia. Three roles, one brain. Decision-making has collapsed.

**Done to death:** habit tracker with "AI insights"; Notion template with extra steps; another Eisenhower-matrix app; "life dashboard" that just aggregates calendars.

#### C3. Fragmented communication across platforms
Coordination now happens across WhatsApp, Telegram, email, Discord, Slack, and other channels. Important information gets buried, duplicated, or missed entirely. **How might we unify fragmented communication into clear, actionable next steps without requiring users to constantly monitor every platform?**

**Archetype:** Nadia, 32, small-event planner. WhatsApp for vendors, Telegram for the client, email for contracts, Notion for ops. Missed a deposit deadline buried in a group chat of 47.

**Done to death:** unified inbox (Shortwave / Canary own this); Slack-summariser bot; cross-platform universal messenger; email-to-task translator.

#### C4. Invisible cognitive labor *(Powered by AIF)*
A significant amount of effort in teams, schools, families, and communities comes from reminding, following up, organising, and emotionally coordinating others. This work is often unrecognised, unequally distributed, and mentally draining — and young women tend to carry more of this work. **How might we reduce invisible coordination labor, stop it from defaulting to women disproportionately, and overall preserve the human quality of relationships and collaboration?**

**Archetype:** Siti, 20, NTU student, default "class rep" for her project team. Organises meetings, chases deliverables, manages drama — while her 4 male teammates ride on her coordination for free.

**Done to death:** chore-splitter app with gender math; emotional-labor tracker with no action loop; "fair-share" leaderboard that just shames people; family-calendar clone.

### Theme D: Health, Longevity & Sustainability

#### D1. Preventive health feels inaccessible
Longevity is often framed as an elite practice involving expensive diagnostics, supplements, or concierge medicine. This leaves most people without practical systems for extending healthspan in daily life. **How might we make preventive health and longevity actionable, affordable, and embedded into everyday routines?**

**Archetype:** Uncle Tan, 47, hawker at Old Airport Road. 14-hour days, BMI 29, borderline pre-diabetic. Polyclinic told him to "exercise and eat less rice." He genuinely doesn't know where to start.

**Done to death:** habit tracker with "longevity score"; supplement subscription box; AI calorie counter; generic wellness chatbot; age-clock quiz.

#### D2. Unsafe experimentation in health optimisation
As more people explore supplements, wearables, diet protocols, and biohacking practices, many rely on fragmented online advice with little context or safety oversight. **How might we support safe, evidence-aware health experimentation without pushing users into misinformation or over-optimisation?**

**Archetype:** Bryan, 26, software engineer. Takes 8 supplements a day based on podcasts. Currently stacking creatine, NAD+, ashwagandha, and a testosterone booster — zero medical supervision.

**Done to death:** "Ask DocGPT" wellness chatbot (Wanting will torch this); supplement-interaction scanner without clinical data; blood-marker dashboard with no ingest pipeline; "bio-age" score app.

#### D3. Poor fit of Western health defaults in Asian contexts
Much of mainstream health advice is built around Western diets, lifestyles, body norms, and work patterns. These assumptions often do not transfer cleanly to Asian populations and everyday realities. **How might we build health and longevity systems that are adapted to Asian genetics, diets, climates, and work cultures?**

**Archetype:** Mei Ling, 35, PM. Fitness app tells her to eat 2,000 kcal and run 10km. Her 1.55m frame, rice-heavy diet, and Singapore humidity make the standard prescriptions counterproductive.

**Done to death:** "Asian diet" recipe app with no personalization; rice-GI calculator as a standalone product; localized skin on MyFitnessPal; "TCM wellness" mood quiz.

#### D4. Hidden burnout among caregivers
Caregivers often absorb physical, emotional, and logistical strain over long periods, yet most products target patients rather than the people supporting them. This creates a hidden resilience problem. **How might we better support caregivers' energy, resilience, and mental health before burnout becomes chronic?**

**Archetype:** Huda, 45, mid-career professional. Three school-age kids, an ageing mother with early-stage Parkinson's, a husband working offshore. Has not slept >6 hours in a month.

**Done to death:** caregiver mood journal; respite-care marketplace (multiple exist); support-group app; "self-care reminders" notification push.

#### D5. Health literacy starts too late
Young people often do not understand how sleep, food, stress, movement, and reproductive decisions compound over time. By the time health becomes salient, many habits and trade-offs are already entrenched. **How might we help young people make informed long-term health decisions earlier, using empowering rather than fear-based systems?**

**Archetype:** Joel, 17, JC1. Averages 4 hours of sleep, 4 bubble teas a week, benches twice a week, one proper meal a day. No idea any of this compounds.

**Done to death:** teen-targeted sex-ed chatbot (school-gated, sensitive); teen mental-health chatbot (Woebot et al. crowded); gamified habit app; "future-you selfie" aging filter.

#### D6. The fertility window vs career acceleration dilemma *(Powered by AIF)*
Young women face rising pressure to optimise career early while managing long-term biological trade-offs. The system forces a choice it never asks of men. **How might we build decision-support tools that give young women realistic, empowering data about long-term life planning — without fear-based narratives?**

**Archetype:** Charmaine, 33, senior associate at a law firm. Partner track in 3 years. IVF cycle would ideally start this year. Nobody in her firm has modelled the trade-off honestly for her.

**Done to death:** fertility tracking app (Flo, Clue, Natural Cycles own this); generic work-life coach; IVF-cost calculator; ovulation-prediction wearable.

### Theme E: Emerging AI Opportunity Space

#### E1. AI-native infrastructure for agent systems
Most software infrastructure today assumes humans are the primary operators. But agentic systems increasingly need to coordinate with other agents, tools, and environments directly. **How might we build infrastructure designed for AI-to-AI interaction, rather than retrofitting human-centered software for agent workflows?**

**Archetype:** Ishaan, 28, indie builder. Has 4 agents that need to talk to each other through Slack + webhooks + a Google Sheet. Plumbing held together with duct tape.

**Done to death:** another agent framework (LangChain, CrewAI, AutoGen crowded); generic "LLM router"; vector-DB wrapper; yet another MCP demo server.

#### E2. Governance, permissions, and auditability for autonomous systems
As autonomous agents take more actions on behalf of users and organizations, questions of oversight, access control, and post-action accountability become foundational. **How might we design agent systems with safety, permissions, and auditability built in by default rather than added later?**

**Archetype:** Jia Qi, 35, compliance officer at a mid-sized SG fintech. Her firm's agents now touch customer data and move money. She has no way to audit what any of them did last Tuesday.

**Done to death:** enterprise "AI governance" dashboard (crowded); policy-as-code demo with no real agent integration; compliance-report generator; "AI red team" service with no product.

#### E3. AI applied to physical systems, not just software
Much of the current AI wave is concentrated in digital productivity, content, and dashboards. But some of the highest-value opportunities may sit in logistics, manufacturing, energy, and construction. **How might we apply AI directly to physical-world systems where coordination, efficiency, and safety improvements have real economic impact?**

**Archetype:** Aunty Mui, 58, runs a hawker stall. Orders ingredients by WhatsApp 3×/week, always over- or under-orders, throws away ~15% of daily prep.

**Done to death:** smart-home demo; generic IoT dashboard; computer-vision safety app with no deployment partner; "digital twin" slide deck.

#### E4. Preserving human agency in AI-mediated environments
As AI enters education, work, healthcare, and public systems, users may become more assisted but less autonomous. The challenge is ensuring AI amplifies human agency rather than replacing it. **How might we design human-facing AI systems that increase human capability, judgment, and agency instead of making people more passive or dependent?**

**Archetype:** Ethan, 16, Sec 4. Uses Claude for every homework question. No longer sure whether he understands calculus or whether Claude does.

**Done to death:** an "AI off" toggle (not a product); dependency detector that just measures usage time; screen-time app with AI branding; "digital detox" retreat.

#### E5. From Prompt User to System Architect *(Powered by AIF)*
As AI tools become easier to use, the real leverage shifts to those who design systems, workflows, and agents. And if the next wave of systems and infrastructure is built primarily by one demographic, its biases get automated at scale. **How might we build AI-native development tools or "agentic playgrounds" that move young women from prompt-engineering to building and directing system architecture?**

**Archetype:** Priyanka, 20, NUS FASS student. Knows prompts well, built a custom GPT once. Has never looked at how an agent is actually wired. Thinks "system prompt" = "the prompt you send the system."

**Done to death:** generic prompt-engineering course; "learn to prompt" chatbot; no-code agent builder with no pedagogy; yet another GPT Store clone.

## Judging criteria

### Challenge-Solution Fit ⭐️⭐️⭐️
*Does the project meaningfully solve the challenge statement? Is the target audience or use case well-defined?*

### Technological Execution ⭐️⭐️⭐️

**For technical teams:**
*Are APIs integrated in a way that adds real value? Is there evidence of custom logic, workflows, or system design? Does the solution go beyond a simple wrapper around an LLM?*

**For non-technical teams:**
*Are AI tools used in a thoughtful and structured way (not just prompting randomly)? Is there a clear workflow or system behind how the solution works? Does the solution go beyond a basic or one-off use of tools (e.g. chaining, automation, multi-step flows)?*

### Product Thinking & UI/UX ⭐️
*Is the user flow and interface clear, intuitive, and easy to navigate? Does the product reduce friction vs current alternatives?*

### Originality & Insight ⭐️
*Is the approach differentiated from typical solutions? Does it reframe the problem in an interesting way?*

### Evidence of Real Demand ⭐️
*Interviews, surveys conducted during the remote week.*

**Scoring math:** 3+3+1+1+1 = 9 points total. Challenge-Solution Fit + Technological Execution = **67%** of score. Product/UX, Originality, Real Demand = tiebreakers. Budget hackathon time accordingly.

## Stacking challenges

Multiple challenge statements can be combined, but they must all be addressed through **one general solution** — not separate features stapled together. The bundled challenges should share a root cause that a single product can credibly tackle.

---

## Market context & edge

Any proposed solution should account for **existing players in the market** — direct competitors, adjacent tools, and incumbents that could absorb the wedge. Map what's already shipping, identify gaps the incumbents won't fill, and articulate the specific **edge** that makes this solution stand out: a framing, primitive, audience, price point, or mechanism they can't or won't copy.

### Framing principles given this panel

2. **Ground every example in SEA.** Six SEA-focused investors + two SG gov bodies reward Hawker-Centre / HDB / kopi-level specificity over generic "users."
3. **If it's an AIF-tagged challenge, say AIF out loud.** Janet is in the room; signal you read the sponsor's actual framing.
4. **For health ideas, prove the pipeline.** Wanting will sniff out wellness fluff — show ingest → inference → recommendation steps, and be honest about what you don't know.
5. **For agent / trust ideas, foreground policy implications.** Sandy will reward real thinking about governance; not a demo of "we logged it."
